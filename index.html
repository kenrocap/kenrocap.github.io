    Secondaries Opportunities

    Secondaries investing opportunity is a derivative of the primary investment category. Venture capital went mainstream in India in mid 2000’s and has developed strongly over the last 20 years. In the 10 years between 2008 and 2017, based on PitchBook and Tracxn data, an estimated $60B+ was invested in venture capital across India and Southeast Asia at an annual average of $6B. Both entrepreneurship and number of VC funds grew in tandem explosively after and between 2018 and 2024, an additional $200B was invested, at an annual average of $35B.

    A key measure of VC success is DPI or distributed to paid in capital and on this metric, the VC funds in the region have low DPIs and large gaps between portfolio value of the Fund vs its realized DPI. This is primarily due to long gestation period for start ups to reach stable business models, immature capital markets, scarce M&A exits and paucity of secondary funds that can bridge the final stage gap in the company’s journey.

    Limited Partners that have patiently backed the VC funds require returns from their earlier investments to reinvest in the industry and to meet their ongoing operating obligations. There is increasing alignment between Limited Partners and Fund managers to increase the pace of capital distribution for the overall health and balance of the industry.

    In addition to VC funds, the other key shareholders in start ups include Founders and employees, public market investors, corporates, family offices and the like. The India and SEA Secondaries Opportunity is estimated at over $100 Billion and growing at over 25% annually.
